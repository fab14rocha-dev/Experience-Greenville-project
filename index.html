Joseph's idea

What Joseph is building

Joseph offers 60-minute immersive, curated experiences designed around each guest's personal world — their favorite sport, song, food, or most meaningful memories. The person being surprised has no idea what's coming. Every detail of the experience is built around who they are.

Experience types — with real examples

Dining — The guest arrives at a restaurant to find their name at the top of the menu, with their favorite foods created as off-menu specials. The team is lined up waiting for them by name. A handwritten card arrives sharing how they first met, with a photo from that moment.

Sports — For a NY Knicks fan: a basketball waiting on the court, red carpet entrance to center court, announced as the star by name, scoreboard showing 101–100 with 7 seconds left. They take the game-winning shot. Then a 5-shot challenge against a friend, family member, or partner, with different point values placed on the court by difficulty.

Music — A private surprise concert featuring the guest's favorite songs, performed live by a singer.

What I'm currently building

Landing page — anniversary and birthday focus
A single page targeting couples aged 21–45. Designed to sell the concept emotionally without revealing the surprise. Sections: hero, what is this, how it works, experience levels ($ / $$ / $$$, no prices shown), and a final CTA. Premium visual feel with smooth animations.
Conversational intake form (full screen overlay)
Opens as a full screen overlay triggered by any CTA button. One question at a time. Back navigation and keyboard support. Confirmation prompt on close to avoid losing answers.

Two paths:
Path A — Planning a surprise for someone else: specific questions about the person being surprised.
Path B — Both want to be surprised: softer, open-ended questions that don't give anything away for either person.
Backend
Form submissions saved to Firebase. Joseph reviews each one manually and follows up with the client. No payment flow for now — manual approval first. An admin view for Joseph to manage submissions is planned as a next step.

Market research

Similar businesses in the US

Three companies operating in the same space. All US-based, all with proven traction. Useful for understanding the market, the positioning, and where Joseph's concept is different.

Pack Up + Go
packupgo.com

A surprise travel company that plans domestic getaways entirely on your behalf. You fill in a profile about your preferences, they handle everything — destination, hotel, activities. You find out where you're going the morning of your trip. Founded in 2016 by a 23-year-old with no outside funding, grown into a multi-million dollar business.

Customers
43,000+ travelers since 2016
Price range
From $500/person (road trip) · $900+ (flights)
Funding
Zero — bootstrapped to multi-million dollar revenue
Model
Fill a profile → they plan everything → surprise reveal
Why it matters: Closest proof of concept to Joseph's model. Same mechanic — you give us information, we handle the surprise. 43,000 customers proves real demand. The key difference: Pack Up + Go sends you somewhere. Joseph builds a moment around exactly who you are. More personal, more premium.
Blind Experiences
blindexperiences.com

A luxury travel agency that started with surprise honeymoons — couples handed over the planning entirely and surprises were revealed through sealed envelopes throughout the trip. Expanded into tailored family and multi-generational itineraries. Operates at the premium end of the market with a strong emphasis on the emotional reveal.

Niche
Honeymoons, anniversaries, luxury family travel
Price range
Premium / luxury tier
Signature detail
Sealed envelope reveals throughout the experience
Model
Full-service planning · Surprise built into every step
Why it matters: Shows that the surprise mechanic works at the luxury end. Also shows that starting with a narrow audience (honeymoons) and expanding later is a valid strategy. Joseph could do the same — start with anniversaries, expand from there. The sealed envelope idea is also a nice detail worth borrowing conceptually.
Bespoke Experiences
bespokeprivatetours.com

A US-based company offering handcrafted, fully personalized experiences in specific cities — Nashville, San Francisco, Chicago, New Orleans. Each experience is built around the client's interests and delivered with a high level of local knowledge and curation. Highly rated on TripAdvisor across multiple city locations.

Model
City-based · Locally curated · Fully personalized
Reviews
Consistently high ratings on TripAdvisor
Audience
Couples, visitors, special occasions
Positioning

Why it matters: This is the closest model to what Joseph is building — a city-based, bespoke experience service. It proves you don't need to be national to be successful. A local, deeply personal offering in one city is a real business. Greenville could be Joseph's version of Nashville.
Local landscape

Competition in Greenville and nearby cities

Research across Greenville, Spartanburg, Columbia (SC) and Charlotte (NC) — the nearest relevant markets.

Key finding
No business in Greenville or any nearby city is offering what Joseph offers. The market is wide open locally. This is a genuine first-mover advantage in his city.
Greenville, SC
No direct competition

What exists in Greenville is entirely generic — escape rooms (Escape Artist Greenville), spa packages (River Falls Spa), culinary tours, cooking classes, paint-and-sip nights, and standard restaurant reservations. Some venues like Hotel Hartness and Table 301 offer elevated experiences, but none are personalized around the guest. No one is doing bespoke, surprise-based experiences built around who the person actually is.

Opportunity: Joseph is the only person in Greenville doing this. The landing page can legitimately say there is nothing else like this in the city.
Spartanburg, SC
~30 min from Greenville

Couples options in Spartanburg are limited to craft breweries, live music venues, antique shops and standard dining. No curated or personalized experience business found. A natural expansion market once the Greenville model is proven.

Note: Close enough to serve without a full expansion. Worth mentioning on the page as a service area once traction is established.
Columbia, SC
~1.5 hrs from Greenville

Columbia has the usual mix — spa packages, dance classes, wine-and-paint studios, and upscale dining. Nothing personalized or surprise-based. The city has a large university population and is the state capital, which means a sizeable couples and anniversary market.

Note: Viable future market. Not an immediate priority.
Charlotte, NC
~1.5 hrs from Greenville

Charlotte has Giftory and XperienceDays — both experience gift catalog platforms. These are marketplace products where you pick from a list of pre-existing activities. They are not personalized and not surprise-based. No bespoke, curated experience business was found operating locally in Charlotte either.

Note: Charlotte is a large, affluent market. If the Greenville model works, Charlotte is the most logical next city — and the competition there is weak.
Experience details

What Joseph actually delivers

Everything Joseph has described so far, organized by experience type. Summarized from his messages — useful for writing copy, designing the form questions, and understanding what each tier might include.

Dining
Most developed concept

A fully staged restaurant experience that makes the couple feel the whole evening was designed around them — because it was.

  • The restaurant's menu is redesigned with the couple's names at the top and their favorite dish listed first — printed to look like the restaurant made it just for them
  • The full restaurant team is lined up at the entrance and greets the couple by name on arrival
  • At select restaurants during off-peak times, a live singer performs their favorite song
  • One partner writes a card in advance — Joseph places it on the table before they arrive as a surprise
  • Pickup from the hotel or parking spot in a themed golf cart or luxury 3-wheel bicycle
  • Red carpet rolled out for the cart, bike, and restaurant entrance
Music
AI + live options

Two ways to deliver a music moment — one tech-driven, one live. Can be used on-site or off.

  • AI-generated personalized song (via Songer) — their names and story woven into the lyrics. Delivered as a surprise during the experience, and kept as a keepsake
  • Live singer performing their favorite song — Joseph has shown examples including a Bon Jovi tribute group and a solo singer surprising a couple mid-experience
Sports
Widest range of activations

The most flexible category — Joseph has built out a full set of physical challenges and activations that work across different ages, skill levels, and occasions.

  • Basketball Last Second Shot — scoreboard shows 3 seconds left, they take the game-winning shot. High drama, big moment
  • Catching Challenge — ball thrown at varying speeds and angles, designed to feel like a championship event regardless of skill level
  • Shooting Challenges — three unique basketball court games built for all skill levels
  • Electric Bike Tour — bikes delivered to the hotel, Falls Park, or Unity Park. Scenic route with surprise photos of the couple planted along the way. Personalized drink stop waiting for them by name
  • Bocce Ball Challenge — a reimagined version of bocce with Joseph's own spin on the format
  • Aerobe — used mostly with families and younger children
  • Speed & Running Challenges — family races designed so that slower runners can still win
  • Personalized Sports Card — custom-designed professional-style sports card used as both a keepsake and a backdrop (primarily for teen birthdays)
Kids & Family Extras
Birthday-focused add-ons

A few specific touches Joseph has designed for younger birthday guests that extend beyond the core three categories.

  • The birthday child is brought their own pizza dough, sauce, and cheese — they make their own pizza at the table and walk it back into the kitchen themselves
  • Personalized sports card as a backdrop and keepsake for the occasion
